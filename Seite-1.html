What is a Decentral Domain?

The part to the right of the "dot" is called the top-level domain (TLD).
The part to the left of the "dot" is called the second-level domain (SLD).

So for the example purple.com, "purple" is the SLD and "com" is the TLD.

In Web2, TLDs are owned by corporations and ownership is centrally controlled.
In Web3, anything can be a TLD owned by anyone.
